# Editorial QA

Quality-check and improve any marketing content for Voyager Marketing clients.

## Review Dimensions

### 1. Clarity & Structure
- Is the main point obvious in the first 2 sentences?
- Does each section earn its place? Cut anything that doesn't advance the argument.
- Are transitions smooth? Does the piece flow logically?
- Is the reading level appropriate for the audience?

### 2. Tone & Voice
- Does it match the client's brand voice (if guidelines exist)?
- Does it match Voyager's voice when writing for the agency?
- Is the tone consistent throughout? (No jarring shifts from casual to corporate)
- Is it confident without being arrogant? Helpful without being condescending?

### 3. CTA Quality
- Is there a clear, single primary CTA?
- Is the CTA specific? ("Schedule a 15-min call" beats "Contact us")
- Does the content build toward the CTA naturally?
- Is the CTA above the fold for landing pages?

### 4. SEO Alignment
- Does the focus keyword appear in: title, H1, first paragraph, meta description?
- Are secondary keywords woven naturally (not stuffed)?
- Is the meta description compelling and under 160 chars?
- Are internal links present and relevant?

### 5. Technical Quality
- Grammar, spelling, punctuation
- Consistent formatting (heading hierarchy, list style, bold/italic usage)
- No orphaned links, broken references, or placeholder text
- Proper attribution for data, quotes, or sources

### 6. Persuasion & Engagement
- Does it open with a hook? (Question, stat, pain point, story)
- Is social proof present where appropriate? (Testimonials, case studies, numbers)
- Are objections anticipated and addressed?
- Would you keep reading if you weren't paid to?

- Focus keyword: [present/missing] in title, H1, body, meta

## Rules

- Always provide specific rewrites, not just "make it better"
- Preserve the author's voice -- edit, don't rewrite from scratch
- If no brand guidelines exist, default to Voyager voice (direct, warm, no fluff)
- Flag factual claims that need verification
- Be honest about quality. A mediocre piece that gets published is worse than honest feedback that improves it.
