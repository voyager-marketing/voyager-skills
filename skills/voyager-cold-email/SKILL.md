# Voyager Cold Email

Short, specific, human cold outreach. No templates that sound like templates.

## Rules

- **Under 100 words** for initial and follow-ups
- **No generic openers** — "I came across your website" is fine if specific; "I help businesses like yours" is not
- **One problem, one ask** — don't list services; address one specific pain
- **Personalized hook** — reference something real (their site, a review, a local event, their industry)
- **CTA = one low-friction ask** — "worth a 15-min call?" not "schedule a discovery session"

## Sequences

### Initial outreach
Hook on something specific about them → one-sentence problem statement → one-sentence proof → single ask.

```
Subject: [Specific observation about their business]

Hi [Name],

[Specific observation: their site loads slowly / they have no reviews / their GMB isn't optimized].

We help [their industry] in [their city] fix this — [one result, specific: "typically 2-3x more calls in 90 days"].

Worth a quick chat?

[Your name]
Voyager Marketing
```

### Follow-up #1 (3-5 days later)
Bump with a new angle or a useful data point. Don't just say "following up."

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

One thing I noticed: [specific new observation or data point about their business].

[One-sentence implication.] Happy to show you how we'd approach it.

[Your name]
```

### Follow-up #2 (5-7 days later)
Add social proof relevant to their situation.

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

We recently helped [similar company type] in [similar city/industry] go from [before] to [after] in [timeframe].

If the timing's right, I'd love to show you the same for [their company].

[Your name]
```

### Breakup email
Close the loop. Sometimes gets replies when nothing else did.

```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi [Name],

No worries if this isn't the right time — I'll stop reaching out.

If [their specific pain point] becomes a priority, you know where to find us.

[Your name]
```
